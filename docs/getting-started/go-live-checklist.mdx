# Go-Live Checklist ✅

Before you flip the switch and let real visitors talk to Konvoq, take 10 minutes to run through this list. It'll save you from embarrassing mistakes on day one.

---

## 🔧 Widget installation

- [ ] The snippet on your live site uses your **production** workspace key — not a dev or staging key
- [ ] There's **only one copy** of the snippet per page (duplicates cause the widget to load twice)
- [ ] The chat bubble appears on your live URL when you open it in an incognito window
- [ ] You've added your production domain in **Widget → Security → Allowed Domains**

---

## 🧠 Knowledge base

- [ ] Your most important pages are scraped and show status `done` in **Knowledge → Scraped Pages**
- [ ] Top FAQs are added — think about the 10 questions your support team answers most
- [ ] You've had a test conversation and Konvoq returned **accurate, grounded answers** (not hallucinations)

:::tip Test like a real user
Open your site in an incognito window, pretend you're a new visitor, and ask 5–10 questions a real customer would ask. Check that the answers are correct and useful.
:::

---

## 🎨 Widget appearance & behavior

- [ ] Assistant name set to something friendly in **Widget → Persona**
- [ ] Welcome message is welcoming and action-oriented
- [ ] Primary color matches your brand in **Widget → Branding**
- [ ] Lead capture is enabled (or deliberately disabled) — configured in **Leads → Settings**

---

## 📧 Notifications

- [ ] Email notifications are configured in **Account → Settings**
- [ ] Test a lead capture — submit the form and verify the email arrives in your inbox

---

## 📊 Usage headroom

- [ ] Check **Dashboard → Usage** — you have headroom on conversations and vector storage
- [ ] You're on the right plan for your expected traffic volume

---

## 🚀 You're ready

Once everything above is checked off, Konvoq is ready for real visitors. Keep an eye on **Dashboard → Inbox** for the first few days — it's a great way to spot gaps in your knowledge base from real questions people are actually asking.
